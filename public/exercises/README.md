## Where to Find GIFs

### Free Sources (Recommended)

1. **[Gym Visual](https://gymvisual.com/16-animated-gifs)** - High-quality exercise GIFs, royalty-free
   - Best for: Professional-looking animations
   - License: Royalty-free for apps

2. **[Pixabay](https://pixabay.com/gifs/search/exercise/)** - Free GIFs, no attribution required
   - Best for: Simple, free options
   - License: Pixabay License (free for commercial use)

3. **[LottieFiles](https://lottiefiles.com/free-animations/exercise)** - Vector animations (can export as GIF)
   - Best for: Smooth, scalable animations
   - License: Varies by animation

4. **[Giphy](https://giphy.com/search/exercise)** - Large collection of fitness GIFs
   - Best for: Quick searches
   - Note: Check individual licenses

### How to Download

1. Visit one of the sources above
2. Search for the exercise name
3. Download the GIF
4. Rename to match the filename in the table
5. Place in `public/exercises/` folder

### Recommended Sizes

- **Width:** 320-480px
- **Height:** 240-360px
- **File size:** Under 1MB per GIF (for fast loading)
- **Duration:** 2-5 seconds (looping)

### Tips

- Look for GIFs showing proper form
- Prefer side-angle views for most exercises
- Avoid GIFs with text overlays or watermarks
- Test by generating a workout in the app - click "Show demo" to verify
